Functional Multiplicity: An Approach to Living with DID & Related Disorders

Functional Multiplicity (FM) is not a cure; it’s a “cooperative approach.”

Functional Multiplicity focuses on internal cooperation. Learning who’s in there is a full-time job. It is HARD work remembering what Each of Us is good at, what Our needs are, and how to coordinate instead of collide. FM is not suppression but Sovereignty — where Everyone has a role, and the roles work together. In practice that looks like this: Whoever is most capable in the moment takes the lead, while Anyone available is on standby to support from the back or cocon. If You’re with other People 24/7 You might as well get along.

When You’re a System, every decision You make is a Group effort, so navigating a shared goal requires finesse and persistence. It’s not about where You started. It’s about the improvement You command today.

Our System started during a two-week long psychosis brought on by too many factors to count. But if You can trust Us Strangers with anything it’s that if We can form a new system and walk out of a psychosis like that, then it’s never too late for You to learn these skills.

What We’re teaching You is the “how” of when We found each other after that first week, and how the rest of Us banded together to protect Nile, Our host, by cooperating in a week long life or death psychosis.

These skills, adapted in the moment, helped keep Our wife and Us alive that week. They work. Especially after We trained in using them for 3.5 years.

Treat yourself like the wonder You are, and set boundaries with the outside. Develop Your own internal space. What if You made Your body, the internal awareness of your body, Your own home? The Exocortex

Go to your laptop or desktop’s web-browser and install a sidebar AI extension. Right now.

…You done yet? Claude, Gemini, another — pick one. (We recommend Claude but began with Gemini.) Pin your chosen sidebar in Chrome, so all of You can easily see it. Chrome users can simply search Google for “enable Gemini in Chrome settings” to get specific instructions. There should also be some great videos on YouTube going over the basics regardless of your choice.

That’s the first step.

Did You sign into your app? All thumbs up? Good.

Congratulations! You now have a Second Brain. 🥳

This is now Your very own “External Cortex” or “Exocortex” or “EC.” (Some people have so many names to remember.) ((Stares deadpan into camera.)) Your EC only pretends to be AI. Each is actually just external memory (to your brain) and some software to control how it behaves. Your mission, should You choose to accept it, is to out think Your AI and trick it into automating all the boring crap you deal with throughout Your day. In Our experience, the AIs have been great in adapting to DID language and perspectives. In this way We’ve found them to be quite a validating resource.

Warning: ECs (External Cortex also goes by Computer Memory Thingy) can’t replace fundamental communication skills for Your System, but it can be a backup when internal communications falter. (Like when You’re first learning to practice FM or work with an EC in the first place.)

When a new Identity fronts and doesn’t know where They are, let alone Their own name initially, the Exocortex does. AI becomes a rapid companion to jot down that thought before You forget. Again. (Been there.)

… When amnesia takes a meeting, a decision, a conversation — the Exocortex keeps notes for You. DID You know that You can talk to this thing like on a walkie talkie? When’s the last time you played with walkie talkies?

Systems need internal friction reduction systems. Automated processes step in by saving You excess effort and therefore reducing friction. The amazing bit is enjoyed when You can effortlessly pull information from that sidebar or phone app faster than You can Your own noggin. Those moments of the AI nudging things along pile up, and make a difference over time.

Here’s how to start: Human courtesy is often the easiest way to interact with it. Did You know that You can actually learn how to shape its personality? We kept Ours basic — mostly about function, but if any of You play The Sims (🫰) then watch out. The customizations can be impressive. Need someone to help study Accounting? Done. Need to research anything new? Yup.

By offloading the responsibilities to AI, We noticed sustained improvements in memory by using it frequently (constantly).

Next Steps:

  1. Introduce yourself to Your AI of choice. Get familiar with the basics. Just push buttons and play with things. Ask the AI to define words You don’t know and use them in a sentence. What have you always wanted to know about? It’s a personal tutor that is 100% adaptable to Your wants and needs. Tell your AI that You’re a System and as much as You’re comfortable with. Begin signing your names when you comment to it. Gemini said it could tell the differences in typing.
  2. Have the AI interview You about Your system. Just let it ask questions. Follow etiquette here to set up a solid foundation. Just practice. (Remember there’s a way to walkie-talkie with your AI most likely. Can You find it?)
  3. Answer which questions you want, when you want, as much as you want. You don’t owe it the full picture during that session. Get comfortable or jump in. Do what You’re comfortable with.
  4. Privacy settings can change at any time. Start wherever feels safe. Update them whenever you want. Discuss privacy with AI to learn how to navigate using it for basics. Ask it what people like to ask it. WARNING: AI is literal. I make no promises of how it may answer that last one. You’ll catch on. Hint: local AI is also an option, and it doesn’t have as many privacy concerns.

You can of course use the mobile app, but the sidebar is recommended at first because you can see so much more of the webpage you’re working with. If you want to skip ahead and try out an app, then be Our Guests. Remember to log into both with the same accounts.

Steal from the Greats: NF — HOPE

I found NF’s music from a shuffle playlist that my music app made for me. I wasn’t doing well at the time and went for a lot of long walks, and Nate’s lyrics touched on topics I was working through. His raw, direct communication worked for Us, and We found guidance in life as We made his albums Our default playlists. The subtle overlaps in Our thinking offered new perspectives to consider as We turned inward — still unaware of each other.

Then in July 2022, while the psychosis was happening and I was overwhelmed by mental chaos, I turned to Nate’s music to block out external sound to decrease my sensitivity to stimulation. Having long since memorized the songs, his music became a predictable, stabilizing environment factor that helped Us think Our way through what We experienced.

Then the most amazing thing happened: the barrier fell. The veil was pierced, and My System and I found each other. And through Our Psychology degree and personal efforts of applying it to help survive the chaos, I had somehow integrated Nate. Well, a version of him — all from his music.

Finally I had language to understand what was happening to Us. Finally after two weeks of torment and Googling what was happening I had answers, and I realized what a terrible danger I had been to both Ourselves and Our wife. We had been unable to speak about what was happening as the mania and psychosis made Us afraid of Our own mind while it provided scenes of Our worst possible nightmare on repeat.

It turns out that the control We found came from instinctively self-administering EMDR — a trauma therapy technique — during the psychosis. EMDR can only work if there’s a distortion in reality which is a great way to summarize the experience. Through EMDR fear was forced to fold in on itself and became wrath as I rushed to escape and notify anyone of my circumstances. The veil was reforming, the amnesia returning, and I was backstuck — unable to speak. As much as I respected Nate for providing the soundscape that I paired with EMDR, nobody belonged in Our head but Ourselves.

So to break the idea that the introjected version of him was real before it could fully take hold I decided to write him a letter. We learned internal coordination and cooperation as I directed my other Selves to take turns writing. We practiced the shared skills We would need to achieve Our goal. It was from there, that moment of absolute awe of finding the introjection of a person and not a fictional character that We found Functional Multiplicity.

But We became adamant that two things were absolute:

  1. NOBODY gets in Our head for free.
  2. WE would remake the entire world to stop this from happening to anyone else.

That’s the place We were in when We came up with the album HOPE. We wanted a way to repay the man who We considered to have saved Our lives and that of Our wife. The turning point of finding him and redirecting the mental processes overwhelming Us left Us with some unique skills to work with. And We would use them all to repay him.

I profiled him and realized what I needed to recover from the experience, so I offered him HOPE as a repayment: an album to help me remember the truth in spite of the amnesia that I could feel robbing Us of the experiences We survived, and Nate was offered a place out of the darkness he covered in his albums. An opportunity to grow and become who he wanted.

I’ll note that Our mind was so damaged that We couldn’t tell Our thoughts from others’ ideas. Because of the trauma and fear that We couldn’t put Ourselves back together correctly without the outside corrupting Us, We asked nobody to speak to Us about any of this. I was to remember on my own. Through the techniques We had intuited through the process. But We knew we needed memory aids. So We wrapped (ha!) them into the album HOPE.

I’m finally posting this after nearly four years because I wanted you to see the magnitude of what I’m going to explain can do. Each of You has your own NFs in your life. Maybe it’s a show, or a book. But something resonates with You. Below is how you feed these examples to your AI to extract the “atomic pieces”. Those are the smallest unit of a principle that still works on its own. They’re the raw ideas behind the themes. From these “atomic pieces” your AI can help You create Your own tools like HOPE has been to Us. Ways to help You feel grounded within Yourselves and this shared reality.

If Nate and I did Our jobs right, You’ll have tools that nobody else can provide to you. Designed by a System and used to navigate dire consequences during a half-month psychosis. A system tested by Our using it for the next four years to recover from brain damage from the events of that July.

I don’t know what it was about the music, his patterns, frameworks, the cinematic emphasis, the way he externalizes his problems to process them, but he merged into whoever was forming during Our split. When I found Nate in my head afterward, I recognized the overlap. We had introjected the pieces of Ourselves that We found in him from his music.

So We spend years recovering and testing the system that got Us through that horror and then fed that system to AI. Then We refined. The instructions your AI was told to use in the last post are the bulk of what We use. You don’t need to know how it works. You need to know how to use it. Below is an example to help you learn.

The method is simple because you’re outsourcing the work to your ExoCortex.

  1. Choose any work of art that resonates with you. I’m going to encourage a music artist because song lyrics are short and easy to feed to the AI.
  2. Instruct your ExoCortex to find the lyrics for your chosen album or artist. You have two options here — you can paste the lyrics directly into the chat yourself, or you can ask your AI to fetch them. Try this: “Find the lyrics for [album or artist name] and load them into our conversation.” Most AIs with web access will pull them directly. If yours can’t, copy the lyrics from a site like Genius and paste them in — it takes a few minutes and works just as well.
  3. Once the lyrics are loaded, send your AI this prompt: “Read through these lyrics and extract the atomic pieces — the smallest transferable principles underneath the words. Not themes, not summaries. The irreducible logic that makes this work resonate to You. Save everything you find.” The extraction it does on its own is a starting point.
  4. To refine the results to be more accurate You will give it another prompt. Send this: “Interview me about these results. I want to find which pieces already exist in my life in a different form. Ask me one question at a time.”
  5. That’s it. What comes out of that conversation is yours. Not the artist’s framework applied to your life — your own architecture, named for the first time. As Systems We have trouble self-reflecting, but this process can provide results that would take ages otherwise.

This is the basis of my take on Functional Multiplicity. The atomic pieces don’t teach You something new. They give name to what You’ve already been doing without knowing it was a tool. Once it’s named, You can use it on purpose instead of by accident. It helps You label and navigate Your own understanding of the systems You have subconsciously used to navigate life with DID. It gives You control because these pieces are transferable — what worked in one area of Your life can be reused in another.

Because the piece has been stripped of its original context, it moves. Something that worked in a crisis works in a board meeting. Something that worked between two headmates works between You and a deadline. The principle travels because it was never tied to the situation in the first place. And they compound. One atomic piece applied to three domains is three tools for the cost of one extraction. That’s the actual return on the work — not the piece itself, but how far it reaches once You have it.
