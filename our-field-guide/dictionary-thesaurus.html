A note on terminology

This language is still being built — by the Communities who live it, not institutions. The terms below are how this site uses it. Yours own lexicon takes precedence.

DIDictionary

System mechanics — how plurality works from the inside.

Functional Multiplicity
A state of high-functioning cooperation and internal democracy among parts without requiring final fusion.
Amnesia Tax
The cognitive, temporal, and metabolic cost incurred by memory gaps and the energy required to bridge them during identity switches.
Distributed Cognition (DCog)
A framework where cognitive processes are spread across internal members, material artifacts (the Exocortex), and the environment.
24/7 Co-Consciousness
The ability for multiple internal members to remain aware and present simultaneously, allowing for concurrent task performance.
Base Operating Layer (BOL)
The fundamental "Good Human" rules and ethical recursion that establish a safety floor for the entire system.
Persona Identity Layers (PILs)
The individual roles, demeanors, and mission-specific identities that sit atop the BOL.
Contextual Lens Files (CLFs)
Specialized background data and scripts triggered by specific environmental cues to inform individual personas.
Pink Noise (1/f) Dynamics
A mathematical signature of metastability and optimal coordination within a plural system.
System Mapping
Visual methods — heat maps, flowcharts, or internal landscapes — used to externalize internal relationships and combat denial.
Asking Inside
A technique of posing mental questions and monitoring for auditory, somatic, or emotional responses to build internal trust.
Apparently Normal Parts (ANPs) & Emotional Parts (EPs)
Terms from structural dissociation theory describing parts focused on daily survival vs. those holding trauma memories.
Somatic Snap
Physical posture flex → focused intent → snap → release tension. Reduces fronting time from seconds to instant. Built during the DID period.
Backstuck
A DID term for a state in which a member is stuck at the back of the system and unable to front.
DID (Dissociative Identity Disorder)
A condition in which two or more distinct identity states — each with their own name, age, personality, and memories — share one body. Formerly called Multiple Personality Disorder. The clinical label most commonly used, though many Systems prefer person-first or community language.
OSDD (Other Specified Dissociative Disorder)
A dissociative condition that shares features with DID — including distinct identity states and amnesia — but doesn't meet every clinical criterion for a DID diagnosis. Equally real, equally valid. Often used by Systems whose experience doesn't fit the narrow DID definition.
Introjection
The process by which a System unconsciously absorbs a person, character, or entity from outside and forms an internal member based on them. The resulting member is called an introject. Can happen with real people, fictional characters, or even concepts.
Psychosis
A state in which a person loses contact with shared reality — experiencing delusions, hallucinations, or severe disorganization of thought. In a DID context, psychosis can interact with switching, flooding, and identity confusion in ways that standard clinical descriptions don't fully account for.
The Veil
On this site: the internal barrier that keeps System members unaware of each other and of the System's own plurality. Not a wall — more like a one-way mirror. The person on one side can't see through it; the System on the other side always could. Lifting the veil means becoming aware. Reforming means the amnesia returning.
EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing)
A trauma therapy technique that uses bilateral stimulation — typically eye movements, tapping, or audio tones — to help the brain reprocess distressing memories. Requires a distortion in reality to work, which makes it structurally compatible with certain dissociative states. Can be self-administered, though this is not standard clinical guidance.
Integration
On this site: the process of building coordination, communication, and shared function among System members — not to eliminate them, but to work together more effectively. Integration does not require or imply fusion. A System can be deeply integrated and remain fully plural. This is the goal Functional Multiplicity is built around.
Stasis
A state in which a System member is neither active nor dormant in the usual sense — suspended, present but not fronting, not communicating, not developing. Sometimes experienced as the member simply not being there for extended periods. Different from dormancy in that stasis implies a kind of pause rather than rest.
Proprioception
The body's internal sense of its own position, movement, and spatial orientation — felt without looking. In an FM context: the practice of turning inward to sense who is present, where tension lives, and what the body is telling You right now. Not dissociation — the opposite. Grounding through awareness of the body You all share.
